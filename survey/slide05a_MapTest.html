Political Mapping–Practice run

In the final part of this survey, you’ll be asked to arrange people on a map based on how similar their political views are. People with similar views should be placed close together, while those with different views should be farther apart.

Before we begin, let’s do a short practice round to help you get used to the task.

You'll start with several dots on the right side. Please drag them one by one onto the canvas, based on the instructions below:

  1. First, place the dot labeled self anywhere on the canvas. This represents your own political views.
  2. Now, place your friend F close to you. You think F has similar views to yours.
  3. Next, add your co-worker C. You often disagree with C, so place them farther away from you.
  4. However, you think C disagrees even more with F than with you — so place C even farther from F.
  5. Now add your sister S. You feel S is quite politically different from you, so place her far from you.
  6. But you also believe S has more in common with both F and C than with you — so place her somewhere closer to them.
